Public Safety & Cybersecurity Advisories

🚨 How to Report Identity Theft or Hacking

If your account has been compromised, follow these immediate steps:

  1. Document Evidence: Take screenshots of the unauthorized activity, emails, or messages.
  2. Secure Accounts: Change your passwords immediately and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  3. Notify Authorities:
    • DOJ Office of Cybercrime: cybercrime@doj.gov.ph
    • DICT CERT-PH: incident@cert.gov.ph / +63 2 8920 0101
    • NBI Cybercrime Division: (02) 8524-8216

🔍 Guidance on Detecting Fake Websites

Scammers often create "look-alike" websites to steal your login credentials.

  • ✅ Check the Domain: Official Philippine government sites always end in .gov.ph. Be wary of .com or .net mimics.
  • ✅ Verify SSL: Look for the HTTPS and the padlock icon. While not a 100% guarantee of safety, the absence of it is a major red flag.
  • ✅ Grammar & Layout: Professional government sites rarely have frequent spelling errors or low-quality, blurry images.

💰 Avoiding Investment Scams

The SEC and DICT warn against "Get Rich Quick" schemes trending on social media.

  • Too Good to be True: If a project promises 100% returns in a week, it is likely a Ponzi scheme.
  • Check SEC Registration: Before investing, verify if the company has a license to solicit investments from the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).
  • Pressure Tactics: Scammers will pressure you to "invest now" before the opportunity disappears.

🛡️ Protecting Your Identity (NPC Guidelines)

According to the National Privacy Commission, you should:

  • Never share your OTP (One-Time Password) with anyone, even someone claiming to be from a bank or government agency.
  • Be careful with "Social Media Challenges" that ask for your birthdate, mother’s maiden name, or first pet—these are common security questions.
  • Limit the amount of personal info you share on public social media profiles.
